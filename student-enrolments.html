Where Are the Students?
Mapping Victoria's Educational Landscape

The Story Behind the Numbers

Victoria's educational landscape is constantly evolving, shaped by demographic shifts, urban development, and changing community needs. This interactive dashboard tells the story of where students are going to school, how enrollment patterns are changing, and what these trends mean for education planning across the state.

Key Insights

Urban Growth

Melbourne's outer suburbs are experiencing unprecedented enrollment growth, with some schools seeing student numbers double in just five years. This rapid expansion highlights the need for proactive infrastructure planning.

Regional Shifts

While some regional areas face declining enrollments, others are experiencing growth due to lifestyle changes and remote work opportunities. This creates both challenges and opportunities for rural education.

School Choice

The data reveals interesting patterns in school type preferences, with significant variations in the popularity of government, independent, and Catholic schools across different regions.

Demographic Changes

Changing population demographics are reshaping school communities, with implications for curriculum planning, language support, and cultural programs.

How We Tell This Story

This dashboard combines multiple data sources to create a comprehensive view of Victoria's educational landscape:

  • Department of Education and Training (DET) enrollment data
  • Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) demographic information
  • School location and classification data
  • Historical trends spanning multiple years

Using Power BI, we've transformed this raw data into an interactive narrative that allows users to explore enrollment patterns across different dimensions:

  • Geographic analysis by Local Government Area (LGA)
  • Temporal trends showing year-over-year changes
  • School type comparisons (Government, Catholic, Independent)
  • Demographic breakdowns by gender and year level
