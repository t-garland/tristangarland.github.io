Does Money Make the Grade?
Analysing the Relationship Between School Funding and Student Success

The Story Behind the Numbers

Education funding is one of the most debated topics in Australian public policy. This analysis dives deep into the relationship between school funding and student outcomes, challenging common assumptions and revealing surprising patterns in how resources translate into educational success.

Key Insights

Funding Efficiency

Some schools achieve exceptional results with modest resources, while others struggle despite significant funding. This analysis identifies the factors that make the difference.

Resource Allocation

How schools spend their funding matters more than how much they receive. Strategic investment in specific areas shows stronger correlation with improved outcomes.

Socioeconomic Impact

The relationship between funding and outcomes varies significantly based on the socioeconomic context of the school community.

Long-term Effects

Investment in early years education shows stronger correlation with long-term student success than funding increases in later years.

How We Tell This Story

This analysis combines multiple data sources to create a comprehensive view of school funding and outcomes:

  • Department of Education funding data
  • NAPLAN results and growth metrics
  • School financial reports
  • Socioeconomic indicators
